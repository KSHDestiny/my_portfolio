### Architecture Is Tradeoffs

System design decisions always balance simplicity, scalability, consistency, delivery speed, and operational overhead.

### Typical Decisions

Common tradeoffs include monolith versus microservices, synchronous versus asynchronous workflows, and stronger consistency versus higher availability.

### Practical Thinking

The best design is the one that solves the actual business problem while remaining operable for the current team.

## Summary

Good architecture is less about perfect patterns and more about choosing the right compromise for real constraints.
